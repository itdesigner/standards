Engineering Standards : Estimation Factors (or T-Shirt Sizing)

Created by Jerry Shaughnessy, last modified on Oct 28, 2017
Requirements Understanding / Definition

Are the business requirements fully defined and have they been validated with users with an appropriate technique, such as walk-throughs, workshops, and independent verification and validation?

Consider the following:

  • Fully defined requirements
  • Business case understood and appropriate success measures defined
  • Deliverables defined

The following guidelines can be used:

  • Low if all 3 conditions are met
  • Medium if 2 of 3 conditions are met
  • High if only 1 of 3 conditions are met
  • Extremely high of none of the conditions are met


Practical Experience and Knowledge

Consider the following criteria regarding knowledge and experience:

  • The project will use a proven approach.
  • This type of project has been done before in Placester.
  • The project will use resources/techniques that have been applied to this type of project before.

The inherent complexity and risk of the project may increase if the project is untried, unique, and/or leading edge.

The following guidelines can be used:

  • Low if all 3 conditions are met
  • Medium if 2 of 3 conditions are met
  • High if only 1 of 3 conditions are met
  • Extremely high of none of the conditions are met


Number of Components

One consideration for complexity estimation is the number of team components involved.

The following guidelines determine the size of a project based on the number of components:

  • Small for 1 to 5 components
  • Medium for 6 to 15 components
  • Large for more than 15 components


Design Complexity

The following guidelines might help you determine the complexity of a design for a project:

  • Low for up to 5 packages, 20 classes, or 20 events
  • Medium for up to 20 packages, 100 classes, or 100 events
  • High for up to 100 packages, 1000 classes, or 1000 events
  • Extremely complex for more than 100 packages, 1000 classes, or 1000 events


Resource Availability

The following guidelines are used to help determine the impact of resources on the effort

Stability of Team

  • Low for teams that have previously worked together, there is low expected turnover on team
  • Medium for teams that have expected turnover during the duration of the effort or have not worked together previously
  • High if team has not previously worked together and there is expected turnover
  • Extremely high if, in addition to High criteria, parts of the team are distributed geographically

Team Allocation

  • Low if team members can all be allocated 80% or higher to effort
  • Medium if team members can be allocated 60%-80% to effort
  • High if team members can only be allocated 40%-60% to effort
  • Extremely high if members can only be allocated 40% or less
